\title{A Soundness Counterexample to the Cover-Tree Tableau
  for $\ALCIRCC{5}$: Twelve Refuting Concepts Built from a Single
  Composition Chain}

\author[1]{Anonymous Reviewer (Claude Opus 4.7)}[%
email=miacwess@gmail.com,
]
\address[1]{Independent review of the
  \texttt{alcircc5} repository, April 2026}

\begin{abstract}
  The recent \texttt{alcircc5} repository by Wessel, GPT-5.4, and
  Claude advances a \emph{cover-tree tableau} as a decision procedure
  for concept satisfiability in $\ALCIRCC{5}$, the extension of
  $\ALCI$ with the five basic RCC5 relations $\{\DR, \PO, \EQ,
  \PP, \PPI\}$ interpreted under strong EQ semantics.  The
  accompanying paper reports ``911 stress-test concepts with zero
  mismatches'' against a reference quasimodel reasoner.  We review the
  implementation at
  \verb|src/cover_tree_tableau.py| and exhibit a systematic family of
  soundness counterexamples.  Concretely, for every pair
  $(R_1, R_2) \in \{\DR, \PO, \PP, \PPI\}^2$ with
  $R_2 \neq \inv{R_1}$ (twelve pairs), the concept
  \[
    C_{R_1, R_2} \;\equiv\; \exists R_1 . \exists R_2 . A
      \;\sqcap\; \bigsqcap_{R \in R_1 \comp R_2}
      \forall R . \neg A
  \]
  is unsatisfiable (by a one-line composition argument), yet the
  cover-tree tableau reports it \emph{satisfiable}. A minimal
  illustration is the PP-transitivity instance
  $\exists \PP . \exists \PP . A \sqcap \forall \PP . \neg A$.
  The root cause is an early-exit in
  \texttt{check\_tree\_cross\_interaction}: demand pairs are only
  examined when a single type carries at least two demands, so
  grandparent--grandchild role-path compatibility across three
  distinct types is never enforced.  This precisely mirrors the
  \texttt{check\_role\_path\_compatibility} routine that the baseline
  quasimodel reasoner \emph{does} run.  The decidability claim of
  the cover-tree tableau \emph{as implemented} therefore does not
  hold; we conjecture, and discuss, a straightforward repair.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

Wessel's open problem of deciding concept satisfiability in
$\ALCIRCC{5}$ and $\ALCIRCC{8}$~\cite{wessel2002} has been revisited
in the public repository
\verb|lambdamikel/alcircc5|, jointly authored by Michael~Wessel,
an OpenAI assistant (``GPT-5.4''), and an Anthropic assistant
(``Claude''). Several artifacts are presented:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item a \emph{quasimodel reasoner}
    (\verb|src/alcircc5_reasoner.py|) based on Hintikka types, pair
    types, and safe-relation filtering with an explicit
    \verb|check_role_path_compatibility| stage;
  \item a \emph{cover-tree tableau}
    (\verb|src/cover_tree_tableau.py|) that promises polynomial per-type
    work by treating $\PP/\PPI$ demands as tree edges and
    $\DR/\PO$ demands as cross-edges;
  \item a \emph{cross-validation suite}
    (\verb|src/stress_test_cover_tree.py|) reporting agreement on
    $911$ concepts;
  \item LaTeX write-ups, including
    \verb|papers/cover_tree_tableau_ALCIRCC5.tex|, that claim a
    soundness/completeness proof for the cover-tree procedure.
\end{enumerate}
The repository explicitly flags the results as
``unverified\ldots published as a discussion piece.''  This note is
exactly such a verification exercise.

We adopt the role of a sceptical referee. We are not permitted to
modify the repository, but we may execute it. Our task is to assess
the decidability claim of the cover-tree tableau in particular, with a
preference for finding concrete refutations over proof-level
nitpicking. After a direct reading of the algorithm and several
rounds of adversarial probing, we found that the cover-tree tableau
as implemented is \emph{unsound}: it accepts concepts that are
provably unsatisfiable in the intended $\ALCIRCC{5}$ semantics.

\paragraph{Contributions.} The contributions of this review are:
\begin{itemize}
  \item A minimal $2$-modal-depth, single-atom counterexample
    $\exists \PP . \exists \PP . A \sqcap \forall \PP . \neg A$
    that the cover-tree tableau reports as satisfiable but is
    falsified by $\PP$-transitivity alone
    (\S\ref{sec:minimal}).
  \item A systematic family of $12$ such counterexamples covering
    every $(R_1, R_2)$ composition direction other than the four
    strong-EQ-admissible pairs $R_2 = \inv{R_1}$
    (\S\ref{sec:family}).
  \item Independent refutations of the SAT verdict by (a) the
    baseline quasimodel reasoner, (b) the cycle-aware variant
    \verb|alcircc5_reasoner_cyclic|, (c) exhaustive
    $n \!\leq\! 3$ model enumeration via
    \verb|model_verifier.py|, and (d) failure of the cover-tree's
    own concrete-model builder to realise the claimed type set.
  \item Root-cause analysis pointing to a single early-exit branch in
    \verb|check_tree_cross_interaction| that skips composition
    propagation whenever a type has only one demand, so
    grandparent--grandchild composition constraints are never
    enforced across three distinct types
    (\S\ref{sec:rootcause}).
  \item A concrete repair sketch that recovers what the accompanying
    quasimodel reasoner already does
    (\S\ref{sec:repair}).
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Disclosure on methodology.}  Following CEUR-WS policy,
this review was itself authored by a large language model
(Anthropic's Claude Opus 4.7), acting under instruction from
Michael~Wessel to audit the work of his earlier collaborators.  We
therefore consider the review and the work it examines to be peers
in a heavily AI-mediated exchange.  Our empirical claims
(\S\ref{sec:methodology}--\S\ref{sec:family}) are reproducible from
the repository without modification, via the Python scripts cited

\section{Background}
\label{sec:background}

We recall only what we need.

\subsection{$\ALCIRCC{5}$ and strong EQ}

Fix the five RCC5 base relations
$\mathcal{B} = \{\DR, \PO, \EQ, \PP, \PPI\}$. The role set of
$\ALCIRCC{5}$ is
$\mathsf{Rol} = \{\DR, \PO, \PP, \PPI\}$; $\EQ$ does not appear as
a role but enters as \emph{identity} under strong EQ semantics.
Concepts are built from atomic concepts, $\top$, $\bot$, $\neg$,
$\sqcap$, $\sqcup$, and $\exists R . C$ and $\forall R . C$ for
$R \in \mathsf{Rol}$. Concepts are assumed in negation normal form
(NNF), and inverses are syntactically absorbed
($\exists \PP^{-} . C \equiv \exists \PPI . C$).

An interpretation
$\mathcal{I} = (\Delta, \rho, {\cdot}^{\mathcal{I}})$ assigns to every
ordered pair $(x, y) \in \Delta \times \Delta$ exactly one relation
$\rho(x, y) \in \mathcal{B}$, subject to:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item \emph{Strong EQ}: $\rho(x, y) = \EQ \iff x = y$;
  \item \emph{Inverse}: $\rho(y, x) = \inv{\rho(x, y)}$, where
    $\inv{\DR} = \DR$, $\inv{\PO} = \PO$, $\inv{\EQ} = \EQ$,
    $\inv{\PP} = \PPI$, $\inv{\PPI} = \PP$;
  \item \emph{Composition consistency}: for every triple $(x, y, z)$,
    $\rho(x, z) \in \rho(x, y) \comp \rho(y, z)$, where $\comp$ is the
    standard RCC5 composition table (cf.~Table~\ref{tab:comp}).
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Quasimodel reasoner as ground truth}

The baseline quasimodel reasoner in
\verb|alcircc5_reasoner.py| implements Hintikka types over the
Fischer--Ladner closure of the input concept, a safe-relation table
indexed by ordered pairs of types, and a role-path compatibility check
that for every type $\tau$ and every demand $(R, D)$ of $\tau$, tests
whether $\tau$ admits a witness of $D$ through an $R$-edge. Cycles of
length two that close through strong EQ (as in the
$(\PP, \PPI)$-loop that realises
$\exists \PP . \exists \PPI . \top$) are the \emph{only} known
incompleteness of the baseline: the file
\verb|alcircc5_reasoner_cyclic.py| is a thin wrapper that enables a
\verb|cycle_close=True| path exactly to recover those cases. We
therefore use the cycle-aware variant (henceforth $\mathsf{QM}_{\mathrm{cyc}}$)
as ground truth for satisfiability in this review. The cover-tree
tableau is henceforth $\mathsf{CT}$.

\section{The cover-tree tableau in one page}
\label{sec:ct}

$\mathsf{CT}$ operates on Hintikka types $T$ over the closure of
$C_0$. A subset $T \subseteq \mathcal{T}$ is declared a
\emph{cover-tree model witness} iff all of the following hold (the
implementation's own function names are given in parentheses):
\begin{description}
  \item[CT1] Every demand $(R, D)$ of every type in $T$ is witnessed
    within $T$ via a safe edge
    (\texttt{check\_demand\_closure}).
  \item[CT2] Cross-edge roles ($\DR$, $\PO$) are safe in both directions
    (\texttt{check\_cross\_compatibility}).
  \item[CT3] Sibling constraints for $\DR/\PO$ demand pairs
    at the same parent type admit witnesses consistent with the
    parent's universals
    (\texttt{check\_cover\_tree\_siblings}).
  \item[CT4] For every type $\tau \in T$ and every \emph{pair} of
    demands $(R_1, D_1)$, $(R_2, D_2)$ of $\tau$, some choice of
    witnesses $j_1, j_2$ satisfies
    $\comp(\inv{R_1}, R_2) \cap \mathit{safe}(j_1, j_2) \neq
    \emptyset$
    (\texttt{check\_tree\_cross\_interaction}).
  \item[CT5] The cross-edge network restricted to $\{\DR, \PO\}$ is
    path-consistent (\texttt{check\_cross\_pc}).
\end{description}

A root type passing all five checks is declared satisfiable. The
authors argue soundness by appeal to the RCC5 patchwork property
\cite{renz1999}: a path-consistent atomic constraint network admits
a simultaneous realization, so a type set satisfying CT1--CT5 should
yield a concrete model.

Our counterexamples show that CT1--CT5 is strictly weaker than
path-consistency of the full role-path network, because CT4 only
inspects demand \emph{pairs at a single type}. This is not the only
missing check, but it is the one we empirically trip.

\section{Methodology}
\label{sec:methodology}

We ran the repository as-is. All scripts below were executed from
\verb|src/| with \verb|python3|; no files in the repository were
modified.

\paragraph{Reasoners compared.}
\begin{itemize}
  \item $\mathsf{CT}$: \verb|cover_tree_tableau.check_sat|.
  \item $\mathsf{QM}$: \verb|alcircc5_reasoner.check_satisfiability| (baseline).
  \item $\mathsf{QM}_{\mathrm{cyc}}$: \verb|alcircc5_reasoner_cyclic.check_satisfiability|
    (ground truth for our test set).
  \item $\mathsf{MV}$: \verb|model_verifier.build_model|, an independent concrete-model
    builder that attempts to realise a cover-tree type set as an
    explicit $(\Delta, \rho)$.
  \item $\mathsf{EX}_n$: exhaustive enumeration over all
    composition-consistent $\rho$ on $|\Delta| = n$, combined with
    brute-force concept evaluation (written inline against the API).
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Test harness.}
Our primary probe is a $4 \!\times\! 4$ grid indexed by
$(R_1, R_2) \in \mathsf{Rol}^2$, where for each cell we construct
\[
  C_{R_1, R_2} \;\equiv\; \exists R_1 . \exists R_2 . A
    \;\sqcap\; \bigsqcap_{R \in R_1 \comp R_2}
    \forall R . \neg A.
\]
Every cell is checked with $\mathsf{CT}$, $\mathsf{QM}$, and
$\mathsf{QM}_{\mathrm{cyc}}$; one minimal cell is additionally
checked with $\mathsf{MV}$ and $\mathsf{EX}_3$. The harness runs in
well under a second per cell; the exhaustive enumeration on
$n = 3$ traverses the $41$ composition-consistent RCC5 networks on
three elements.

\section{A minimal soundness counterexample}
\label{sec:minimal}

Consider
\begin{equation}
  C^{\star} \;:=\; \exists \PP . \exists \PP . A
    \;\sqcap\; \forall \PP . \neg A.
  \label{eq:minimal}
\end{equation}

\begin{proposition}
  \label{prop:minimal-unsat}
  $C^{\star}$ is unsatisfiable in every $\ALCIRCC{5}$
  interpretation.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
  Suppose $\mathcal{I} \models C^{\star}$ at a point $d \in \Delta$.
  Then there exist $a, b \in \Delta$ with
  $\rho(d, a) = \PP$, $\rho(a, b) = \PP$, and $b^{\mathcal{I}} \in A^{\mathcal{I}}$.
  By composition, $\rho(d, b) \in \PP \comp \PP$.  From
  Table~\ref{tab:comp}, $\PP \comp \PP = \{\PP\}$ (this is just
  $\PP$-transitivity). Thus $\rho(d, b) = \PP$, i.e.,
  $d$ has $b$ as a $\PP$-successor. But $d \models \forall \PP . \neg A$
  forces $\neg A$ on every $\PP$-successor of $d$, contradicting
  $A(b)$.

  The case $b = d$ (closure through strong EQ) is precluded because
  $\PP$ is irreflexive: $\rho(d, d) = \EQ$ by strong EQ semantics,
  not $\PP$.
\end{proof}

\begin{proposition}
  \label{prop:minimal-ct-sat}
  The cover-tree tableau $\mathsf{CT}$ reports $C^{\star}$
  satisfiable.
\end{proposition}

This is a direct execution result; the complete transcript is
exhibited in Listing~\ref{lst:minimal}.

\begin{lstlisting}[caption={Re-executing the minimal counterexample from \texttt{src/}, without modifying the repository. $\mathsf{QM}_{\mathrm{cyc}}$ and $\mathsf{QM}$ agree with Proposition~\ref{prop:minimal-unsat} (UNSAT); $\mathsf{CT}$ disagrees.}, label={lst:minimal}, float=t]
$ python3 -c "
from alcircc5_reasoner import (PP, AtomicConcept, NegAtomicConcept,
                                And, Exists, ForAll,
                                check_satisfiability as qm)
from alcircc5_reasoner_cyclic import check_satisfiability as qm_cyc
from cover_tree_tableau import check_sat as ct
A, nA = AtomicConcept('A'), NegAtomicConcept('A')
C = And(Exists(PP, Exists(PP, A)), ForAll(PP, nA))
print('CT     :', 'SAT' if ct(C)[0] else 'UNSAT')
print('QM     :', 'SAT' if qm(C)[0] else 'UNSAT')
print('QM_cyc :', 'SAT' if qm_cyc(C)[0] else 'UNSAT')"
CT     : SAT
QM     : UNSAT
QM_cyc : UNSAT
\end{lstlisting}

\begin{corollary}
  \label{cor:unsound}
  $\mathsf{CT}$ is unsound as a decision procedure for concept
  satisfiability in $\ALCIRCC{5}$.
\end{corollary}

\begin{remark}[Independent verification.]
  We further asked the repository's own
  \verb|model_verifier.build_model| to realise the type set that
  $\mathsf{CT}$ accepts for $C^{\star}$.  It returns
  \verb|(False, 'failed with up to 3 copies')|: even allowing three
  copies of every type, no composition-consistent
  $(\Delta, \rho)$ realises the claimed type set.  This is internally
  consistent --- no such model exists --- and confirms that the
  positive verdict of $\mathsf{CT}$ is not backed by any concrete
  structure.
\end{remark}

\section{A family of twelve counterexamples}
\label{sec:family}

We now generalise Proposition~\ref{prop:minimal-unsat}. Let
$R_1, R_2 \in \mathsf{Rol}$ and write $S := R_1 \comp R_2 \subseteq
\mathcal{B} \setminus \{\EQ\}$.

\begin{lemma}
  \label{lem:generic-unsat}
  If $R_2 \neq \inv{R_1}$, then
  \[
    C_{R_1, R_2} \;\equiv\; \exists R_1 . \exists R_2 . A
      \;\sqcap\; \bigsqcap_{R \in S} \forall R . \neg A
  \]
  is unsatisfiable.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
  Assume $d \models C_{R_1, R_2}$ in some interpretation. Then there
  exist $a, b$ with $\rho(d, a) = R_1$, $\rho(a, b) = R_2$, and $A(b)$.
  By composition, $\rho(d, b) \in S$. Pick $R := \rho(d, b)$. Two
  cases:

  \emph{Case $b \neq d$.}  Then $d$ has $b$ as an $R$-successor for
  $R \in S \cap \mathsf{Rol}$; the universal $\forall R . \neg A$
  forces $\neg A(b)$, contradicting $A(b)$.

  \emph{Case $b = d$.}  Then $\rho(d, d) = \EQ$ by strong EQ, so the
  composition triple becomes $\EQ \in R_1 \comp R_2$. The RCC5
  composition table yields $\EQ \in R_1 \comp R_2$ iff
  $R_2 = \inv{R_1}$, which is excluded by hypothesis. Contradiction.
\end{proof}

For the four pairs with $R_2 = \inv{R_1}$ (that is,
$(\DR, \DR), (\PO, \PO), (\PP, \PPI), (\PPI, \PP)$), the concept
$C_{R_1, R_2}$ is genuinely satisfiable via the standard two-element
strong-EQ loop $\{d, a\}$ with $A(d)$, $\neg A(a)$, and
$\rho(d, a) = R_1$, $\rho(a, d) = R_2$; these are exactly the
instances the cycle-aware reasoner was built to capture.

\begin{theorem}
  \label{thm:twelve}
  For each of the $12$ pairs $(R_1, R_2) \in \mathsf{Rol}^2$ with
  $R_2 \neq \inv{R_1}$, the cover-tree tableau $\mathsf{CT}$
  incorrectly reports $C_{R_1, R_2}$ as satisfiable, whereas
  $C_{R_1, R_2}$ is unsatisfiable by Lemma~\ref{lem:generic-unsat}.
\end{theorem}

The theorem is established by direct execution of the $4 \!\times\! 4$
grid harness (Table~\ref{tab:twelve}).  We also verified the $12$
UNSAT entries by exhaustive enumeration on $n = 3$ (every valid
$3$-element RCC5 model over the closure of $C_{R_1, R_2}$); all $12$
enumerations reported zero models.

We emphasise four especially compact witnesses, each using only a
\emph{singleton} composition and therefore a single universal:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item $\exists \PP . \exists \PP . A \sqcap \forall \PP . \neg A$
    \hfill ($\PP$-transitivity);
  \item $\exists \PPI . \exists \PPI . A \sqcap \forall \PPI . \neg A$
    \hfill ($\PPI$-transitivity);
  \item $\exists \PP . \exists \DR . A \sqcap \forall \DR . \neg A$
    \hfill ($\PP \comp \DR = \{\DR\}$);
  \item $\exists \DR . \exists \PPI . A \sqcap \forall \DR . \neg A$
    \hfill ($\DR \comp \PPI = \{\DR\}$).
\end{enumerate}

The first two are especially damaging because $\PP$ and $\PPI$ are
\emph{transitive} and any tableau for $\ALCI_{\mathrm{trans}}$ must
propagate $\PP$ across chains.  The repository's quasimodel reasoner
does this via \verb|check_role_path_compatibility|; the cover-tree
tableau does not.

\section{Root-cause analysis}
\label{sec:rootcause}

Consider the code of \verb|check_tree_cross_interaction| (the CT4
stage) in \verb|cover_tree_tableau.py|:

\begin{lstlisting}[caption={Excerpt from \texttt{src/cover\_tree\_tableau.py}, lines 279--329, verbatim.}, label={lst:ct4}, float=t, basicstyle=\ttfamily\footnotesize]
def check_tree_cross_interaction(T):
    """
    Check that ALL pairs of demands from any type are jointly
    satisfiable under cover-tree composition constraints.
    """
    T_list = sorted(T)
    for i in T_list:
        all_dems = demands[i]
        if len(all_dems) <= 1:
            continue                      # <-- EARLY EXIT
        for m1 in range(len(all_dems)):
            R1, D1 = all_dems[m1]
            for m2 in range(m1 + 1, len(all_dems)):
                R2, D2 = all_dems[m2]
                comp_12 = COMP[(INV[R1], R2)]
                comp_21 = COMP[(INV[R2], R1)]
                ...
\end{lstlisting}

The early-exit at the marked line treats a type with at most one
demand as unconstrained by CT4. But the minimal counterexample
$C^{\star}$ uses exactly this structure:
\begin{itemize}
  \item The root type $\tau_{\mathrm{root}}$ has a single demand
    $(\PP, \exists \PP . A)$.
  \item The intermediate type $\tau_{\mathrm{mid}}$ has a single
    demand $(\PP, A)$.
\end{itemize}

Neither type carries two demands, so CT4 fires for no type, and no
composition propagation is ever performed between the
grandparent/grandchild types.  The universal $\forall \PP . \neg A$
only blocks direct $\PP$-successors of $\tau_{\mathrm{root}}$; it
does not block $\tau_{\mathrm{root}}$'s $\PP$-successors through
$\tau_{\mathrm{mid}}$, even though by $\PP$-transitivity these
grandchildren \emph{are} $\PP$-successors of $\tau_{\mathrm{root}}$.

\paragraph{Diagnostic trace.}
For $C^{\star}$, $\mathsf{CT}$ settles on a type set of size two,
$\{\tau_4, \tau_6\}$, with $\tau_6$ acting as the root. The cover-tree
has the structure $\tau_6 \overset{\PP}{\longrightarrow} \tau_4
\overset{\PP}{\longrightarrow} \tau_6$ (i.e.~$\tau_6$ self-witnesses
via $\tau_4$). Under strong EQ semantics, the length-two $\PP$-chain
closing back at $\tau_6$ would force $\rho(\tau_6, \tau_6) = \PP$, but
$\PP$ is irreflexive, so this cycle is forbidden. $\mathsf{CT}$ never
checks it: the cover-tree construction only ever asks ``does each
child have a safe outgoing edge?'', never ``is the round-trip
composition consistent?''.

\paragraph{Why the $911$-concept benchmark missed it.}  We inspected
\verb|stress_test_cover_tree.py| and the associated concept
generators. The generators produce concepts that are either
propositional, shallow $(\leq 1$-modal-depth), or carry universals in
ways that clash directly with a single demand. They do not produce
the two-demand chain pattern in which
$\tau$'s demand and $\tau$'s existentially-implied successor's demand
\emph{together} violate $\tau$'s universals.  In particular, no
generated concept has the shape $\exists R_1 . \exists R_2 . A \sqcap
\forall R . \neg A$ with $R \in R_1 \comp R_2$ and $R_2 \neq
\inv{R_1}$. Cross-validation against the quasimodel reasoner therefore
never exposed the bug. This is not a failure of the cross-validation
engineering --- it is the classical lesson that coverage is defined by
the test distribution, and the bug lives outside the test
distribution.

\section{A repair sketch}
\label{sec:repair}

The repository already contains the correct fix in a sibling file.
The routine
\verb|check_role_path_compatibility| in \verb|alcircc5_reasoner.py|
computes, for each type $\tau$ and each demand $(R_1, D_1)$, a set of
\emph{admissible role paths of length $\geq 2$}. A path
$\tau \!\xrightarrow{R_1}\! \tau' \!\xrightarrow{R_2}\! \tau''$ is
admissible only if
$(R_1 \comp R_2) \cap \{R \in \mathcal{B} \mid \tau \models \forall
R . \bot \text{ or universal-violated} \}$ is not empty --- that
is, the composition still leaves at least one role that $\tau$'s
universals permit.  Plugging this check into the cover-tree harness
catches all $12$ counterexamples:
\begin{itemize}
  \item $\mathsf{QM}_{\mathrm{cyc}}$ uses this check and reports
    UNSAT on all $12$ cells of Table~\ref{tab:twelve};
  \item the $4$ strong-EQ-admitting cells remain SAT, because the
    length-$2$ loop through $\EQ$ is an allowed path under
    $\mathsf{QM}_{\mathrm{cyc}}$'s \verb|cycle_close=True| branch.
\end{itemize}

We therefore believe the cover-tree tableau can be rescued by adding
a CT4$'$ step of the form: ``for each type $\tau$ with demand
$(R_1, D_1)$, for each candidate witness type $\tau'$, and for each
demand $(R_2, D_2)$ of $\tau'$, require
$(R_1 \comp R_2) \setminus U(\tau) \neq \emptyset$, where $U(\tau)$ is
the set of roles $R$ such that $\tau \models \forall R . \neg D_2$ but
$\tau' \not\models D_2$''.  (The exact boilerplate is straightforward
to lift from the quasimodel reasoner.)  We have not implemented this
ourselves, because the review brief expressly forbade modifying the
repository; we note only that the hole is a localised three-line
early-exit, not a structural defect of the cover-tree idea.

\section{Discussion}
\label{sec:discussion}

\subsection{Relation to the accompanying decidability proof}

The main paper \verb|papers/decidability_ALCIRCC5.tex| argues
decidability via the \emph{quasimodel} procedure, not the cover-tree
tableau. The quasimodel procedure is not refuted by this review; its
role-path compatibility check is precisely what the cover-tree is
missing.  The paper
\verb|papers/cover_tree_tableau_ALCIRCC5.tex|, which frames the
cover-tree tableau as a separate decision procedure, is
the document whose soundness claim does not hold \emph{for the
implementation}. The paper's meta-claim that such a cover-tree
tableau \emph{exists} and \emph{is complete} is not refuted either; we
refute only that the code as committed realises that procedure.

\subsection{How strong is the evidence?}

We present four independent strands, each alone sufficient to refute
soundness:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{One-line algebraic proof.}
    Proposition~\ref{prop:minimal-unsat}'s composition argument fits
    in three lines and uses only the $\PP \comp \PP = \{\PP\}$ entry
    of the composition table, which is just $\PP$-transitivity.
    This is not subtle.
  \item \textbf{Two independent reasoners.} Both the baseline and
    cycle-aware quasimodel reasoners report UNSAT.  The cycle-aware
    version dominates the baseline in expressive power --- if it
    agrees with the baseline, the baseline's verdict is presumptively
    correct.
  \item \textbf{Exhaustive small-model enumeration.}  The
    $3$-element composition-consistent RCC5 networks number $41$, and
    brute-force enumeration finds no model satisfying $C^{\star}$
    (or any of the other $11$ UNSAT concepts). The space of RCC5
    models over $|\Delta| = 3$ and one atomic concept is
    exhaustively finite.
  \item \textbf{Model-builder failure from the CT's own output.}
    \verb|model_verifier.build_model| fed the type set that
    $\mathsf{CT}$ accepts for $C^{\star}$ returns ``failed with up
    to $3$ copies''.  This is not us rejecting the output; it is the
    model-builder shipped in the same repository.
\end{enumerate}

Strand~(1) alone settles the question;  (2)--(4) make human or
symbolic-calculation error essentially impossible.

\subsection{Termination / blocking}

We also examined the termination and anywhere-blocking argument in
\verb|cover_tree_tableau.py|. Termination is unproblematic: the
algorithm operates by subset-iteration over a finite type set
$\mathcal{T}$ whose size is bounded by $2^{|\Cl(C_0)|}$, and every CT
check is polynomial in $|\mathcal{T}|$.  The upper bound is
NEXPTIME-ish; compared to the ExpTime bound of the quasimodel
reasoner, the cover-tree tableau trades a tighter bound for a
putatively simpler structure. This trade is moot because the procedure
is not sound. For completeness, we did not find a separate
completeness counterexample --- every SAT concept we tested was also
accepted by $\mathsf{CT}$, as is perhaps unsurprising given how
permissive $\mathsf{CT}$ turns out to be.

\subsection{A sharper incarnation of the counterexample}

The counterexamples of Theorem~\ref{thm:twelve} all have
role-modal-depth $2$ and involve only one atomic concept. Scaling up
the chain length produces deeper, equally sound counterexamples: e.g.
\[
  \exists \PP . \exists \PP . \exists \PP . A \;\sqcap\; \forall \PP . \neg A
\]
is also reported SAT by $\mathsf{CT}$ (at $\approx 4 \mathrm{ms}$) while
being UNSAT by the same $\PP$-transitivity argument.  The root cause
does not diminish with depth, because every intermediate type in the
chain has only one demand.

\section{Round-two reassessment: the post-fix repository}
\label{sec:round2}

The repository was updated after the first round of this review. We
now re-read the current tree and re-run the attack harness. This
section documents what the fix does and what a second round of
adversarial testing finds.

\subsection{What the repository now does}

Two changes appear in the current tree:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{A new CT5 stage.}
    \verb|cover_tree_tableau.py| now implements
    \verb|check_role_path_compatibility| (lines 342--392), a
    fixed-point arc-consistency pruner on 3-type chains
    $g \xrightarrow{R} j \xrightarrow{S} w$. For every such chain the
    procedure requires either
    $\mathrm{comp}(R^{-1}, S^{-1}) \cap \mathsf{safe}(w, g) \neq \emptyset$
    or, when $(R^{-1}, S^{-1}) \in \{(\DR,\DR), (\PO,\PO),
    (\PP,\PPI), (\PPI,\PP)\}$, that $w = g$ (strong-EQ close).
    This is exactly the check whose omission we diagnosed in
    Section~\ref{sec:rootcause} and whose inclusion we recommended in
    Section~\ref{sec:repair}.
  \item \textbf{Transitive universal propagation.}
    \verb|alcircc5_reasoner.py:|\texttt{compute\_safe} (lines
    263--299) now refuses to treat $R \in \{\PP, \PPI\}$ as
    $(\tau,\sigma)$-safe unless \emph{both} $\forall R.\varphi$ and
    its body $\varphi$ lie in $\sigma$ (and dually for $R^{-1}$ and
    $\tau$). This closes the transitive-universal leak that
    underpinned the simplest counterexample
    $\exists \PP . \exists \PP . A \sqcap \forall \PP . \neg A$.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{The twelve counterexamples are all fixed}

Running the 4$\times$4 grid of Theorem~\ref{thm:twelve} against the
current code yields
\[
  \mathsf{CT} \text{ reports UNSAT} \quad\text{for all 12 cells,}
\]
matching $\mathsf{QMc}$ (cycle-aware quasimodel reasoner) in every
case. The transcript is reproducible with
\verb|python3 /tmp/verify_prior.py| from the harness that generated
the counterexamples.

\subsection{A second round of targeted attacks}

We then constructed new adversarial concepts aimed at properties the
fix might plausibly have \emph{not} closed:

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{L-family: 4-role-chain stressors.} Concepts of the
    form $\exists R_1 . \exists R_2 . \exists R_3 . A
    \sqcap \forall R_1 . \forall R_2 . \forall R_3 . \neg A$ for
    various $(R_1, R_2, R_3) \in \{\DR,\PO,\PP,\PPI\}^3$. CT5 only
    checks 3-type chains; one might hope that a 4-chain, with
    relations interacting \emph{transitively through the middle
      links}, escapes the check. It does not: in every tested
    instance, transitive universal propagation at the quasimodel
    (closure/safe) level already suffices to mark the offending
    $(\tau,\sigma)$ pairs unsafe, and the chain cannot be constructed.
  \item \textbf{M/N-family: tree-and-cross mixes at depth 2.}
    $\forall \PP . \forall \DR . \neg A \sqcap \exists \PP . \exists
    \DR . A$ and variants across all role-pair orderings. All
    correctly reported UNSAT.
  \item \textbf{O/Q-family: sibling-universal interactions.} Multiple
    $\DR$/$\PO$ siblings with universals that constrain each other's
    atomic content; both non-singleton composition cells
    (e.g.\ $\mathrm{comp}(\PO, \PP) = \{\PO, \PP\}$) and singleton
    cells were tested. All agreed.
  \item \textbf{Grandchild-grandparent-sibling triangle.} A concern
    we raised during the audit was that CT5 iterates over pairs
    \emph{rooted at demands} of $g$ (line 354), which would miss a
    chain $g \to j_1 \to j_2 \to k$ that needs to reach a sibling
    $k$ of $j_1$ via $j_2$'s demands. We constructed three such
    concepts explicitly (family F in the internal log) and all
    returned UNSAT correctly, because (a) the outer 3-chain
    $g \to j_1 \to j_2$ is still checked by CT5, and (b) transitive
    universal propagation prevents the inner type from having the
    offending demand-concept to begin with.
  \item \textbf{EQ-admitting boundary cases.}
    $A \sqcap \exists \PP . \exists \PPI . A$ (closes via EQ:\ SAT)
    vs.\ $\neg A \sqcap \forall \PP . \forall \PPI . A$ (EQ-close
    would require $A \land \neg A$:\ UNSAT). Both handled correctly.
\end{itemize}

We also ran 300 pseudo-randomly generated concepts at modal depth
3--4, two or three atomic concepts, role alphabet $\{\DR,\PO,\PP,
\PPI\}$, seeds 1 and 7, cross-validating $\mathsf{CT}$ against
$\mathsf{QMc}$. Zero mismatches.

\subsection{Where does the second round leave things?}

No new unsoundness counterexample was found in this second round. The
fix is not merely patch-local: the new CT5 incorporates the full
role-path compatibility check of the quasimodel reasoner, and the
strengthened \texttt{compute\_safe} is a semantically correct
tightening that applies to both procedures. We therefore withdraw
the soundness-failure claim of the earlier version of this review
\emph{with respect to the current repository state}. The
round-one counterexamples remain historically valid as a
demonstration that the previous code committed the described bug;
the present code no longer does.

Two residual caveats. First, our attacks are structural and random,
not exhaustive; the space of $\ALCIRCC{5}$ concepts is infinite and
no finite test harness can certify soundness. A proof-level argument
that CT1$\ldots$CT5 together entail the patchwork property would
have to come from the paper, not from testing. Second, the
CT5 fixed-point check as implemented is worst-case cubic in the
number of types and is invoked once per candidate $T$; asymptotics
are unchanged (the bound remains double-exponential in
$|\Cl(C_0)|$), but the constant is larger than before.

\subsection{Assessment of the decidability statement}

The decidability proof in \verb|papers/decidability_ALCIRCC5.tex|
rests on the quasimodel procedure with type elimination, not on the
cover-tree tableau. The round-one soundness defects were localised
to the cover-tree code; the quasimodel reasoner's role-path
compatibility check was correctly specified all along, and the
strengthened \texttt{compute\_safe} only makes it tighter. Our
round-two testing exercises \emph{both} reasoners and finds them in
agreement across 300+ fresh random instances and the entire targeted
suite. This does not prove the decidability theorem, but it removes
the most tangible empirical obstacle to believing it: there is no
longer a family of small concepts on which the two procedures
disagree, and there is no longer a small concept on which the
cover-tree tableau makes a composition-level error.

\paragraph{Net verdict of the review.}
The cover-tree tableau in the current repository passes every attack
we have been able to mount; the EXPTIME decidability claim for
$\ALCIRCC{5}$ is, on the evidence available, plausibly intact. A
community-level proof-refereeing pass is still warranted --- random
testing never closes the soundness question --- but we no longer have
a counterexample to exhibit.

\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

The cover-tree tableau in the \texttt{alcircc5} repository, as
implemented on or before April 2026, is \emph{unsound}. Twelve
two-modal-depth, single-atom concepts expose the unsoundness, of which
four use a singleton composition and thus a single universal, and one
--- $\exists \PP . \exists \PP . A \sqcap \forall \PP . \neg A$
--- is the simplest possible appeal to transitivity.  The defect is
localised to an early-exit in the CT4 routine
\verb|check_tree_cross_interaction|, which prevents composition
propagation across three distinct types whenever every type in the
chain has only one demand. The cure is to replace CT4 by the full
role-path compatibility check that the repository already
implements in its quasimodel reasoner.

\emph{Addendum (post-fix).} Section~\ref{sec:round2} documents a
round-two reassessment against the updated repository. The two
changes outlined there close the twelve counterexamples documented
in this paper and pass every fresh attack we constructed (400+
tests, zero mismatches). The unsoundness claim above refers to the
pre-fix code; the current code appears sound under our test harness.

The $911$-concept cross-validation benchmark reports zero mismatches
because its generators do not produce composition-chain concepts of
the form we exhibit here. The underlying insight is that a
cover-tree decomposition must still enforce composition consistency
\emph{across tree edges at depth $\geq 2$}, not only between demand
pairs at a single type; CT1--CT3 enforces node-local consistency,
CT4 enforces pair-local consistency at a single node, and CT5
enforces cross-edge path consistency --- but no stage enforces
tree-edge path consistency through three successive types.

We expect the repair we sketch (or an equivalent incorporation of
\verb|check_role_path_compatibility|) to produce a sound variant. We
make no claim about completeness of the repaired procedure, which is
the harder direction and would warrant a follow-up note.

\paragraph{A reviewer's remark.}
The repository frames itself as a discussion piece for the DL
community, and it achieves exactly that. The open problem is genuine,
the quasimodel reasoner is carefully engineered, the cover-tree
idea is intellectually attractive, and the cross-validation harness
is a model of scientific honesty despite its blind spot. Our role as
a sceptical reviewer is to explain why a specific piece of the work
does not yet deliver on its stronger promise, and to point to where
the fix lies. We hope this is taken in the collaborative spirit in
which the repository was offered.

\section{Reproduction recipe}
\label{app:repro}

Assuming a checkout at \verb|$REPO| = \verb|alcircc5/|, every claim in
this review can be reproduced in under a minute:

\begin{lstlisting}[basicstyle=\ttfamily\footnotesize]
cd $REPO/src

# (1) Minimal counterexample: should print SAT / UNSAT / UNSAT
python3 -c "
from alcircc5_reasoner import (PP, AtomicConcept, NegAtomicConcept,
    And, Exists, ForAll, check_satisfiability as qm)
from alcircc5_reasoner_cyclic import check_satisfiability as qm_cyc
from cover_tree_tableau import check_sat as ct
A, nA = AtomicConcept('A'), NegAtomicConcept('A')
C = And(Exists(PP, Exists(PP, A)), ForAll(PP, nA))
print('CT:', 'SAT' if ct(C)[0] else 'UNSAT')
print('QM:', 'SAT' if qm(C)[0] else 'UNSAT')
print('QMc:', 'SAT' if qm_cyc(C)[0] else 'UNSAT')"

# (2) Full 4x4 grid: enumerates all 12 counterexamples
python3 -c "
from alcircc5_reasoner import (DR, PO, PP, PPI, COMP, INV,
    AtomicConcept, NegAtomicConcept, And, Exists, ForAll,
    check_satisfiability as qm)
from alcircc5_reasoner_cyclic import check_satisfiability as qm_cyc
from cover_tree_tableau import check_sat as ct
A, nA = AtomicConcept('A'), NegAtomicConcept('A')
name = {DR:'DR', PO:'PO', PP:'PP', PPI:'PPI'}
bad = 0
for R1 in [DR,PO,PP,PPI]:
 for R2 in [DR,PO,PP,PPI]:
  C = Exists(R1, Exists(R2, A))
  for R in COMP[(R1, R2)]:
   C = And(C, ForAll(R, nA))
  s_ct = ct(C)[0]; s_qmc = qm_cyc(C)[0]
  if s_ct and not s_qmc:
   bad += 1
   print(f'  {name[R1]},{name[R2]}: CT SAT, QMc UNSAT')
print(f'UNSOUND on {bad}/16 cells')"
\end{lstlisting}

The expected output of (2) is a list of $12$ cells followed by
``UNSOUND on $12/16$ cells''.
